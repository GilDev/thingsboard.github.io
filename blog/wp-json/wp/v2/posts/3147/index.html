Introduction <\/h4>\n\n\n\n

Equipment in production plays an important role in achieving maximum efficiency, quality, and payback. The issues that are key to optimizing the use of equipment are:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \n
  • Underutilization of Equipment<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Ineffective Production Scheduling<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Excessive Wear and Tear on Machinery<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Operational Bottlenecks<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Suboptimal Maintenance Scheduling<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Misaligned Capacity Planning<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

    Solving these problems requires constant monitoring of equipment utilization and performance metrics. As production output increases, manufacturers may need to partially or completely change their equipment maintenance strategies and schedules to maintain efficiency. Unfortunately, these problems cannot be solved once and for all. But it also means that we are able to endlessly improve the optimization process. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    That is why it is important to have the most up-to-date data when choosing a optimization strategy. Previously, with the help of logging, you could see how the equipment worked at the end of the day or week. With the growth of technology, with the help of IoT devices, we can receive raw data in real-time. With advanced analytics, you can now turn raw data into meaningful reports and predict future equipment behavior using machine learning models.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
