Description of the problem<\/h4>\n\n\n\n

While working with many cases of our clients, we faced unusual problems in solar farms. We decided to show you how it can be solved with the help of advanced analytics.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Solar farms are stations with a large working area, the frequent inspection of which is not financially profitable. That\u2019s why companies conduct quarterly technical inspections on a schedule or in case of serious problems that can be noticed with the naked eye.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

However, many localized problems can significantly affect efficiency in the long run but cannot be noticed simply by comparing a few indicators. For example, the occurrence of corrosion on a solar panel will reduce the solar panel\u2019s efficiency more and more every day. However, the production rate in this case will never reach 0, so primitive detectors will not report the problem. Based on this, the costs will reach from 10 to 40% of the electricity for each day from the string until the upcoming quarterly inspection. We talked more about them in the article about finding anomalies in solar power plants.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Knowing what these problems are and where they appear, it is only necessary to build the right search mechanism to answer the question: \u201cIn which segment of the solar plants did the error occur?\u201d. In other words, it will tell not only about the problem\u2019s existence but also about its location.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Usually, solar panels are located in approximately the same area, so they are all covered by the same conditions and usually have similar power generation, with a 4-5% difference. The reason for the fluctuation is that solar panels operate in uncontrolled environmental conditions. The power generation of solar panels may vary depending on the temperature, degree of pollution, and humidity, which may differ during the day for each solar panel separately.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
